# 🌱 Amplicon Week 2025

## A Complete Guide to 16S, ITS, 18S, 12S, and COI Metabarcoding

### December 8–13

• By Jojy John

Welcome to **Amplicon Week 2025**, a structured, educational, and practical series designed to teach amplicon sequencing and metabarcoding workflows from start to finish.

This project includes daily blog posts, LinkedIn summaries, reproducible code, installation scripts, marker gene primers, and end-to-end pipelines covering:

- **16S rRNA** (bacteria & archaea)
- **ITS** (fungi)
- **18S rRNA** (microeukaryotes)
- **12S rRNA** (vertebrate eDNA)
- **COI** (animal DNA barcoding)

The aim is to provide a single, open, and comprehensive resource for anyone working with amplicon data—students, researchers, ecologists, and computational biologists.

---

## 🎯 Project Goals

- Teach amplicon sequencing fundamentals
- Provide reproducible workflows for all major marker genes
- Share real scripts used in microbial ecology research
- Integrate QIIME2, R (Microeco / DADA2), PICRUSt2, Tax4Fun2, ITSx, and web tools
- Build a reusable open-source project, published as **v1.0** at the end of the week

## 🗓 **Day 1 — Dec 8**

### _Introduction to Amplicon Sequencing & Metabarcoding_

**Topics:**

- What are 16S, ITS, 18S, 12S, COI?

- Primer sets, sequencing platforms
- Understanding ASVs, OTUs, taxonomy tables

## 🗓 **Day 2 — Dec 9**

### _QIIME2 Setup + Databases + Classifier Training_

**Topics:**

- Activating QIIME2
- Creating metadata
- Importing FASTQ files
- Databases: SILVA (16S), UNITE (ITS), PR2/MIDORI (18S), BOLD/NT (COI)
- Training classifiers (`fit-classifier-naive-bayes`)
- Outputs: demux → quality plots → denoised ASVs
